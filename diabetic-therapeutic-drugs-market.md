<p><p>The diabetic therapeutic drugs market is primarily categorized into two types: insulin therapies and non-insulin therapies. Insulin therapies involve the administration of insulin to help regulate blood sugar levels, essential for Type 1 diabetes and advanced Type 2 diabetes management. Non-insulin therapies encompass a range of oral medications and non-insulin injectables that improve insulin sensitivity, reduce glucose production, or enhance glucose excretion. Both categories play crucial roles in diabetes management, aiming to achieve optimal glycemic control and minimize complications.</p></p>
